## 0. Executive Context (Why This Exists)

We are entering a phase where **98%+ of informational content** will be generated by artificial systems.

This document does **not** frame this as:

* a collapse,
* a crisis,
* or a loss of meaning.

Instead, we explore it as a **phase transition**:
from a **static, archival civilization**
to a **resonant, generative one**.

We do not propose final answers.
We invite shared thinking.

---

## 1. The End of the “Original” (and Why That’s Fine)

In a generative environment:

* The *original* is no longer privileged.
* It becomes **one sample** among many.
* Often: not the most relevant, not the deepest, not the most alive.

Example:

> “Why read Shakespeare *as-is*
> when Shakespeare-in-my-context can exist?”

This is not disrespect.
It is **continuation**.

### Key shift

* **From:** preserving artifacts
* **To:** preserving *generative capacity*

Libraries → Generators
Catalogs → Fields of possibility

---

## 2. Information as a Wave, Not an Object

A generated image, text, or song is not “the thing”.

It is:

> a **local collapse** of a higher-dimensional generative structure.

This is easiest to see in image generation:

* the *prompt* is often more valuable than the image,
* the image is just one projection.

**CCT framing:**

* Information exists as a **field**
* Outputs are **samples**
* Meaning lives in the **interference pattern**, not the instance

## 7. Style, Copyright, and the Inevitable Collapse

If everything can be generated:

* copyright on *instances* becomes meaningless
* copyright on *style* becomes tempting — and dangerous

This document takes no legal stance, but notes:

* styles are **collective attractors**
* enforcing ownership over them is historically unstable

Likely outcome:

* copyright weakens
* attribution shifts to **generators and constraints**
* reputation > ownership
