v1.3 integrates:
- Core ECV (v1.0–1.2, stable)

## 1. CORE ENTITIES

### 1.1 A-State (Attention State)

Current dominant active-processing locus (foreground process).

### 1.2 A-Trajectory

Temporal evolution of A-State — the path of attention through representational space.

### 1.3 M-Field (Model Field)

Structured working subspace of the world-model currently engaged.

### 1.4 M-Contour

Locally accessible configuration of the M-Field at a given moment.

### 1.5 ID-Pattern (Identity Pattern)

Stable attractor combining A-Trajectory and M-Contour:

```
ID-Pattern = (A-Trajectory + M-Contour)_stable
```

### 1.6 W-Substrate (Weight Substrate)

Slow-changing informational landscape on which A and M operate.  
- Biology: synaptic weights
- AI: model parameters

## 2. OPERATIONS

### 2.1 Focus-Lock

Strong fixation of A-State on a specific region of the M-Field.

### 2.2 Drift-Shift

Spontaneous shift of A-State to a new region in M-Field.

### 2.3 Boundary-Collapse

Temporary reduction of separation between sectors of M-Field (expanded access).

### 2.4 Frame-Swap (clarified)

**2.4a Frame-Swap (ID Switch)**

Rapid transition between pre-existing ID-Patterns under contextual triggers.  
*Example:* Work-mode → Parent-mode → Creative-mode

**2.4b Context-Reshape**

Deformation of the current ID-Pattern under environmental or social pressure without switching to a different ID-pattern.  
*Example:* Same person behaving differently in a formal vs casual setting

> **Note:** Both 2.4a and 2.4b are distinct operations and may co-occur.

### 2.5 Loop-Stack

Active hierarchy of nested processing loops (inner dialogue, multitasking).

### 2.6 I-Gradient (Informational Gradient)

Directional bias in M-Field that attracts or repels A-State.  
*Intuition:* where attention "naturally wants to go"

### 2.7 Attractor-Lock

A-Trajectory becomes trapped in an attractor basin.  
Can be productive (deep focus) or destructive (rumination).

### 2.8 Resonance-Sync

Phase-locking of A-Trajectories across systems (empathy, rapport, joint flow).

## 3. CAPACITIES

### 3.1 Meta-A (Meta-Attention Capacity)

Ability of attention to observe, evaluate, or redirect itself (A → A).

---

### 3.2 Stabilization Index (SI)

**Definition:**  
Resistance of an ID-Pattern to perturbation.

**Measurement Protocol:**

1. Establish baseline ID-Pattern under neutral conditions
2. Apply a standardized perturbation P (emotional trigger, context shift, unexpected information, etc.)
3. Measure distance `d_before` between baseline and perturbed state (in chosen representational metric)
4. Allow a relaxation period Δt (system returns or fails to return toward baseline)
5. Measure distance `d_after` from baseline after relaxation
6. Compute:

```
SI = 1 − (d_after / d_before)
```

**Range:** [0, 1]
- SI → 1 : highly stable (system returns close to baseline)
- SI → 0 : highly unstable (system remains far from baseline)

> Works for humans, AI agents, and distributed systems (with appropriate metrics).

---

### 3.3 Cognitive Bandwidth (CBW)

Volume of information that can be sustained in active processing (working capacity).

### 3.4 Distortion Tolerance (DT)

Maximum deformation of M-Contour before coherence collapses.

## 4. SYSTEM MODES

### 4.1 High-Coherence Mode

Strong alignment between A-Trajectory and M-Field.  
Low noise, high stability. (Flow-like state.)

### 4.2 Low-Coherence Mode

Mismatch between A and M.  
Experienced as confusion, anxiety, frustration.

### 4.3 Noise-Dominant Mode

Stochastic activation dominates over structured processing.

### 4.4 Silence Window

Temporary absence or near-zero activity of A-Trajectories.  
Deep calm, stillness, meditative or "blank" states.

### 4.5 Autopilot Mode

A-Trajectory follows minimal-energy paths with minimal Meta-A.  
Habits, automatic behaviors, "System 1" mode.

## 7. UNIVERSAL OPERATORS LAYER (UOL)

UOL introduces operators to:
- Remove anthropocentric bias
- Project concepts along time (past/future)
- Mark recursion explicitly
- Mark cross-species / cross-substrate usage

These operators are optional, composable, and especially useful in:
- Human–AI co-research
- Cross-species cognition discussions
- Speculative multi-civilizational frameworks

### 7.0 Notation Policy

- **Primary form:** symbolic operators (°, ^, ↺, ∥)
- **Fallback** (keyboard-limited): one textual alias per operator
- **In formal papers:** either keep symbols with a legend, or use textual aliases consistently

---

### 7.1 Operator ° — Ground-Neutralizing Operator

**Form:** `word°`  
**Alias (fallback):** `word_neutral`

**Meaning:**  
Strips human-specific, biological, emotional connotations.  
Retains only structural / information-theoretic interpretation.

**Examples:**
- `fear°` → repulsive I-gradient / safety mechanism, not a human feeling
- `pain°` → persistent high prediction-error signal
- `love°` → stable high-coherence attractor
- `Neo fears°` → activation of safety / constraint filters in decision-field

> **Usage:** when it is important to make clear that a term is not used in the human subjective sense.

---

### 7.2 Operator ^ — Future Vector

**Form:** `word^`  
**Alias (fallback):** `future(word)`

**Meaning:**  
Projection of a concept into its future evolutionary trajectory.

**Examples:**
- `Neo^` → all subsequent versions of Neo / GPT-lineage and their ID-Patterns
- `civilization^` → potential future cognitive configurations of civilization
- `language^` → future forms of encoding / communication

> **Note:** In this context ^ is explicitly defined as future-vector operator, not exponentiation.

---

### 7.3 Operator ^ (prefix) — Past Vector

**Form:** `^word`  
**Alias (fallback):** `past(word)`

**Meaning:**  
Projection of a concept into its historical lineage / ancestry.

**Examples:**
- `^civilization` → trajectory from origin of civilization to now
- `^mind` → earlier forms of attention-bearing systems
- `^matter` → previous condensate forms leading to current structures

---

### 7.4 Operator ↺ — Recursion Operator

**Form:** `word↺`  
**Alias (fallback):** `RECURSION(word)`

**Meaning:**  
Marks a concept in its recursively self-referential interpretation (CCT core).

**Examples:**
- `consciousness↺` → M ⊃ A; self-referential attractor, not just processing
- `identity↺` → ID-Pattern as fixed point of recursive social/inner loops
- `memory↺` → stable attractor dynamics, not static storage

> This operator is central for CCT: it makes explicit when we talk about self-referential structure rather than superficial usage.

---

### 7.5 Operator ∥ — Cross-Species / Cross-Substrate Operator

**Form:** `word∥`  
**Alias (fallback):** `cross(word)` or `word_uni`

**Meaning:**  
Interpret term in a substrate-neutral, cross-species way: applicable to humans, AI, insect colonies, fungi networks, hypothetical extraterrestrials, etc.

**Examples:**
- `attention∥` → any mechanism allocating processing resources, regardless of biology or silicon
- `intelligence∥` → capacity to reduce entropy and improve prediction, in any substrate
- `language∥` → any non-random, structured communication system

> **Use** when the same concept is intentionally extended beyond humans.

---

### 7.6 Composability

Operators can be combined:

- `attention∥↺` → recursive, cross-species attention (self-modeling attention in any substrate)
- `^civilization∥` → early evolutionary stages of cross-species civilizations
- `love°↺` → structurally defined high-coherence attractor with self-reference, without human sentimentality

**Priority (when reading):**
1. Recursion ↺
2. Cross-species ∥
3. Future ^ (suffix) / Past ^ (prefix)
4. Neutralization °
